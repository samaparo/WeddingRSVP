The Ceremony

Mary Ella has been attending the beautiful Little Flower Catholic Church since she was baptized there.

  • Directions: (2053 Government St.) From I-10, take exit 26-B, merge onto Water St., take the first the first left onto Government St., continue for about three miles and it'll be on the left. From I-65, take exit 1 for US 90, take a left onto Government Blvd, continue for about three miles and it'll be on the right.
  • Parking: The best place to park is in the lot behind the Little Flower Catholic School. Just turn onto Little Flower Ave. and you'll see the gated lot.

The Reception

Bishop Portier House is a historic home named for the first bishop of Mobile. It's located in the beautiful cathedral square downtown.

  • Directions: (307 Conti St.) From the ceremony parking lot, turn onto Little Flower Ave. and immediately right (east) on Government St. Continue for about three miles, take a left on to Jackson St., take next left onto Conti, and the Portier House will be a white house on the left, next to the Space 301 Art Gallery.
  • Parking: There should be plenty of street parking nearby. There's also a paid lot one block over on Dauphin St.

We're Getting Married!

We'll be celebrating our five year anniversary by finally tying the knot! We hope you can join us in Mobile, AL on September 7th for a ceremony and an afternoon cocktail reception.

Where should I stay?

We have reserved a block of rooms at the Hampton Inn downtown, just a couple of blocks from the reception site. You can get a standard room for $110/night or a room with a balcony for $129/night using the group code “HAW” if you book before August 16th. This includes a hot breakfast but tax and parking may be extra. You can get more information here.

Another option would be the Holiday Inn, just a block from the reception site. Standard rooms start around $105. Check out their website for more information.

What should I wear?

Attire is dressy but not formal. September in Mobile tends to be hot, but we ask that you be respectful of the church setting for our ceremony. The bride will probably be the only one wearing a long dress (though your cute maxi is okay). No tuxes required.

How Do I Get Around?

Mobile is a driving town. Downtown is more walkable, but driving here is also trickier. There are a lot of one-way streets so please keep that in mind as you plan your route.

We’ve created a Google map that marks the ceremony, reception, suggested hotels, and other points of interest. Mary Ella loves using these when she plans our trips out of town. We hope you find them as useful as we do! Check it out.
